Organizational Structure

  • Expand to 18 ALS stations, adding two new Bravo units for a total of 20 ambulances. The three new stations will be strategically placed using GIS and call volume data, with Bravo units positioned in high-call-volume sectors.
  • Increase the number of Field Training Officers (FTOs) to 7 per shift, allowing for 1 FTO per 5 ambulances. The 5th, 6th, and 7th FTOs will act as roaming EMS supervisors, assisting on critical calls and providing training as directed.
  • Develop an EMS Special Operations Team (Critical Care Unit) to handle high-acuity patients requiring specialized care and transportation.
  • Expand personnel assigned to ambulances to three, including two paramedics and one EMT. This staffing model will initially target high-call-volume units and aim for system-wide implementation to enhance patient care and reduce reliance on engine crews for critical patients.

Staffing and Training

  • Enhance the apprenticeship program to include a greater focus on physiology, pathophysiology, and pharmacology.
  • Maintain a minimum of 2 EMS Training Captains and 3 EMS Training Lieutenants to oversee training programs.
  • Offer advanced courses, such as Critical Care Paramedic, Advanced Trauma Life Support (ATLS), and Neonatal Resuscitation Program (NRP).
  • Facilitate Hands-On Training (HOT) or scenario-based exercises to reinforce learning.
  • Revise and improve the QA/QI process, conducting monthly meetings to address system performance and protocol adherence.
  • Establish a Medical Guidelines Review Board to align current protocols with best practices. This board will meet monthly until the guideline revision process is complete.

Operational Efficiency

  • Maintain regular scheduled lifecycle replacements for apparatus and equipment.
  • Acquire ventilators, IV pumps, and LifePak 35s to modernize and enhance patient care capabilities.
  • Transition to blue helmets for personnel in the Rescue Sergeant position. This will facilitate identification of lead EMS personnel on complex scenes.

Community Engagement and Feedback

  • Standardize timelines for complaint investigations and resolutions, ensuring consistent and efficient handling of concerns.
  • Establish more routine community outreach programs such as:
    • School visits.
    • Job fair booths.
    • Informational booths at identified community events.

Financials and Sustainability

  • Allocate budget for:
    • Personnel expansion.
    • Training and education.
    • Technological upgrades.

Summary

Over the next five years, the EMS system aims to evolve into a more robust, efficient, and community-focused organization. By expanding stations, enhancing training, and improving operational efficiency, the system will address current challenges and meet growing demands. These advancements will ensure high-quality emergency medical services while fostering resilience and adaptability for the future.

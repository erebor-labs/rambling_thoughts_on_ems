The structure of our current Emergency Medical Services (EMS) system is as follows:

  • 15 Ambulances:
    • These ambulances are staffed using a dual-medic configuration. A Sergeant acts as the lead medic on each unit.
    • Sergeants assigned to ambulances typically have 3-5 years of field experience. They are often paired with personnel who are either newly hired or recently certified as paramedics, providing an opportunity for mentoring and skill development.
  • 4 Field Training Officers (FTOs) Per Shift:
    • The FTOs are responsible for evaluating and training newly certified paramedics, ensuring they meet the department's performance and clinical standards.
    • They also oversee Quality Assurance and Quality Improvement (QA/QI) initiatives to maintain high standards of patient care and operational efficiency.

These elements work together to form the backbone of our EMS operations, ensuring readiness, reliability, and continuous professional development.
